I'm Rodrigo Sicarelli, a Staff Software Engineer on Stone's Mobile Platform and one of Brazil's
leading voices in Kotlin Multiplatform — Google KMP Acceleration Program (one of five projects
worldwide), KotlinConf 2025 speaker. My job, in one line: I build the platforms that multiply
other engineers.

The story starts in São Paulo in 2011, building ERP software as an intern and learning Android by
pairing with senior devs at a small taxi startup. From there I worked my way up through Brazil's
mobile scene — Walmart, ZAP Imóveis, Easy Taxi, Elo7 — shipping apps through the years when
Android went from afterthought to front door. At 99, Brazil's ride-hailing giant, I led the
Passenger app's Android team and then became the technical bridge for the mobile integrations
with Didi Chuxing after the acquisition — my first taste of engineering across organizations,
languages and time zones.

That taste turned into seven years of international work. In Amsterdam at Chama I swung between
tech lead and engineering manager, treating logistics as a systems problem — we cut average
delivery time from 40 to 10 minutes. In Valencia at Creditas I led cross-country teams and
delivered the org's Server-Driven UI engine, so product teams could ship without app releases.
Back in Amsterdam (remotely) at Polaroid, I rewrote the Bluetooth layer that took the flagship
app's rating from 3.5 to 4.6 and built a hardware platform adopted across four products. The
pendulum between managing teams and deep technical work was deliberate: each side taught me what
the other couldn't.

In late 2023 I came back to Brazil, to Stone, to solve a problem no one had cracked at that
scale: a cross-platform migration architecture for a large fintech. I delivered it in four
months; it was adopted org-wide and took code sharing from 0% to 80%. The scope grew from there —
selective CI that cut costs by 87%, app cold start from 50s to 15s, an observability platform
with 52 metrics, architecture governance through RFCs, and an agentic coding platform (Claude
Code, MCP) used by engineers and PMs alike. Today I operate as Cross-Staff across the three
platform squads, because I believe the highest-leverage code is the code that makes everyone
else faster.

Along the way Stone's KMP work was featured on the Android Developer Blog and at Google I/O 2025,
and I keep giving back to the ecosystem as an open-source maintainer — `fakt`, `kmp-targets`,
`kmp-native-flavors` — and as a technical writer. The structured CV below has the full record.

digital downloads, memberships) — to be added LATER, but architected so I don't have to re-platform.

Survey the full 2025–2026 landscape for monetizing a primarily static/git-deployed personal site,
covering, without pre-judging:
- Payment & merchant-of-record platforms: Stripe, Lemon Squeezy, Paddle, Gumroad, Polar,
  and others — including which act as merchant of record (handling global VAT/tax) vs raw processors.
- Brazil-specific considerations: accepting payment from Brazilian buyers (Pix, boleto, BRL),
  selling internationally in USD, tax/invoicing/compliance for an individual in Brazil, and how
  merchant-of-record platforms change the tax burden.
- Models: one-off digital products, paid courses, gated/premium articles, subscriptions/memberships,
  pay-what-you-want — and which platforms/tools fit each.
- Content gating/auth on a static or hybrid site: how to protect paid content (serverless functions,
  edge auth, signed URLs, lightweight auth providers, course/membership platforms) without breaking
  the static + auto-deploy model.
- Course delivery specifically: self-built (video hosting + gating) vs hosted course platforms
  (Teachable, Podia, Kajabi, self-hosted, etc.) embedded in or linked from the site — trade-offs.
- What minimal architectural choices made NOW (e.g. hosting that supports serverless/edge functions)
  keep the cheapest path open to add payments later.

ROLE: Act as a principal web architect producing an integrated, vendor-neutral landscape report
to help me choose a coherent end-to-end setup for rsicarelli.com.

Produce a SYNTHESIS across all of the following dimensions, showing how choices in one area
constrain or enable the others (the goal is a coherent stack, not isolated picks):

1. Web technology & rendering approach (static site generators, full-stack frameworks, hybrid).
2. "Git as CMS" + auto-deploy: how content in a GitHub repo becomes a live, bilingual site
   with zero manual deploy.
3. Hosting & delivery (CDN, edge, free tiers) and total cost of ownership.
4. Bilingual (pt-BR/EN) architecture with browser-locale defaulting.
5. SEO (Google) + GEO/AEO (AI engines) readiness.
6. Privacy-friendly, free/open-source analytics.
7. A forward-compatible path to selling paid content later (without re-platforming).
8. Design/UI system fit (component model, theming, accessibility).

DELIVERABLES:
- 3 to 4 complete, internally-consistent "reference stacks" (e.g. Stack A / B / C), each described
  end-to-end across all 8 dimensions, with total monthly cost (USD + BRL), effort to launch, and
  the type of person/use-case each suits best.
- A weighted decision matrix scoring each stack against: developer ergonomics, performance,
  SEO+GEO, bilingual support, auto-deploy simplicity, extensibility to payments, cost, and lock-in.
- Explicit trade-offs and the single biggest risk of each stack.
- A recommended sequence of the deeper topic-specific research I should run next.

Follow the unbiased mandate and output contract from the context block. Survey the full 2025–2026
landscape; do not anchor on the most hyped option.

FOCUS: Making the site rank on Google AND be discovered/cited by AI engines (ChatGPT, Claude,
Perplexity, Gemini). Treat classic SEO and GEO/AEO (Generative/Answer Engine Optimization) as
two related but distinct goals.

Research current (2025–2026) best practices and the full toolset for:

Classic SEO:
- Technical SEO for static/hybrid sites: crawlability, clean URLs, canonical tags, XML sitemaps,
  robots.txt, structured data (Schema.org: Person, Article/BlogPosting, BreadcrumbList, Event,
  CreativeWork, SoftwareSourceCode for OSS).
- Bilingual SEO: hreflang for pt-BR/EN, locale URL strategy, avoiding duplicate-content issues,
  and how browser-locale defaulting interacts with crawlers.
- Core Web Vitals & performance as ranking factors; how to measure and keep them green.
- Metadata, Open Graph / Twitter cards, and rich previews.
- Authority/E-E-A-T signals for a real expert (linking talks, OSS, publications, employer).

GEO / AEO (AI engines):
- How ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Gemini crawl, retrieve, and CITE web content in 2025–2026;
  which crawlers/user-agents matter and how to allow them (robots.txt directives).
- The role and current real-world adoption of `llms.txt` / `llms-full.txt` and other emerging
  conventions — is it worth implementing, and how?
- Content structuring that makes a page easy for LLMs to extract and quote (clear headings,
  FAQs, summaries, structured facts, machine-readable data).
- How to become a cited source for queries about KMP / mobile platform engineering / the author.
- Measuring AI-referral traffic and citations (what's actually possible today).

DELIVERABLES: a prioritized, actionable SEO+GEO checklist for launch and ongoing, plus the
recommended (preferably free/open-source) tools to audit and monitor both.
